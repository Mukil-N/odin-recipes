Briyani Recipes


Description

Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic South Asian rice dish made with basmati rice, spices, and typically meat (such as chicken, beef, goat, lamb, or fish) or vegetables.

It is often cooked in layers with saffron, herbs, and garnished with fried onions, boiled eggs, and nuts.

The dish is known for its rich taste and complex flavor profile, making it a popular and beloved meal in many parts of the world.

Ingrediants


    Main Ingrediants

  • Basmati Rice: 2 cups
  • Chicken: 500 grams

    • For Marination

  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Turmeric Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Red Chili Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Garam Masala: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste

    • Whole Spices

  • Bay Leaves: 2
  • Cinnamon Stick: 1-inch piece
  • Cardamom Pods: 4
  • Cloves: 4
  • Black Peppercorns: 6-8

    • For Cooking

  • Onions: 2 large (thinly sliced)
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium (chopped)
  • Green Chilies: 2-3
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves: 1/2 cup (chopped)
  • Fresh Mint Leaves: 1/2 cup (chopped)
  • Ghee or Oil: 4 tablespoons
  • Saffron: a pinch
  • Biryani Masala: 1-2 teaspoons
  • Lemon Juice: 1 teaspoons

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Coat chicken pieces and marinate for 1 hour.
  2. Prepare the Rice: Rinse and soak rice for 30 minutes. Boil with whole spices until 70-80% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Fry onions in ghee/oil until golden. Add tomatoes, green chilies, and cook until soft. Add marinated chicken and cook until done. Stir in coriander and mint leaves.
  4. Layer the Biryani: In the pot with chicken, add a layer of rice. Drizzle saffron milk and lemon juice. Repeat with remaining chicken and rice. Top with fried onions, cashews, and raisins.
  5. Dum Cooking: Cover with a tight lid. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes.
  6. Serve: Gently mix layers and serve hot. Garnish with boiled eggs and additional fried onions if desired.
